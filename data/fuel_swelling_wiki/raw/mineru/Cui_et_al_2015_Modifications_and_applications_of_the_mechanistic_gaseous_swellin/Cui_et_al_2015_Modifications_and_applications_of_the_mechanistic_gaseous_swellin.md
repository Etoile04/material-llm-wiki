## ABSTRACT

Modifications are introduced to the mechanistic gaseous swelling model for UMo fuel.Analytical expressions are formulated to lower the computation cost.Parametric studies have been carried out to reveal the influence of intergranular resolution rate,hydrostatic pressure,and grain-boundary diffusion enhancement factor.The modified mechanistic swelling model is incorporated to perform the finite element （FE） simulation.This mechanistic model demonstrates comparably high eficiency toward the empirical swelling model.The simulated results by the mechanistic swelling model yield good agreement with the measured data.

## 1. Introduction

Fission gas is first generated by the fission event.As gas accumulation proceeds,gas atoms are nucleated and trapped to form and grow small bubbles inside the grain [1]. Meanwhile,energetic fission fragments cause the re-dissolving of the gas from the intragranular bubbles.This phenomenon has been well-studied in UO2 fuel.Irrespective of either heterogeneous or homogeneous,it could be characterized macroscopically by a resolution rate [2]. Similarly, the intergranular resolution model has also been proposed to account for the gas resolution from grain boundary bubbles [3- 5].As for UMo fuel,the intragranular resolution has been included in its fission gas mechanism [6-9]. Once intragranular resolution is included,it becomes physically reasonable to include the plausible intergranular resolution as well. Although this consideration might create difficulty in solving the pertinent gas diffusion equation, it contributes to the completeness of the gas mechanism. Since UMo fuel normally operates to high burnup under low temperature，irradiation-induced recrystallization is prone to happen [10].The recrystallization process results in submicron grains and thus highly affects the fission gas swelling [6]. The small sub-grains decrease the diffusion distance and increase the grain boundary area per unit volume.The gaseous swelling is thus accelerated by the grain recrystallization to have a ‘knee' in its curve. Therefore,this gas mechanism is of great importance.The gaseous swelling model with progressive recrystallization in UMo fuel is proposed by Rest [6]. The only different treatment in this work is the omission of a volumetric factor in the expression of intergranular gas density for the recrystallized grain area.As for mechanical influence,evidence indicated that the hydrostatic pressure could affect the gas bubble swelling [11-15]. Ideally, UMo fuel meat is considered sealed by Al alloy cladding,allowing no fission gas release to free volume under normal operation. Thus,the gaseous swelling is the main consequence of the gas accumulation.The pressure imposed on gas bubbles could be substantially high. Once the pressure dependence is introduced, the gaseous swelling would be associated with the local stress state.

Classified by the methodology,there are two approaches to modeling the gas bubble swelling in UMo fuel. One is the empirical model proposed by Kim and Hofman [1O] and the other is the mechanistic model proposed by Rest [6].A big advantage of the empirical model is its simplicity. Yet, the fitted relation is highly subject to the specific experimental conditions.Many parameters like temperature,grain size and fission rate could not be reflected. In contrast,the mechanistic model readily includes the influence of those parameters.Most importantly,the mechanistic model covers a series of vital gas mechanisms to form a complete theory framework.Undoubtedly,a shortcoming of the mechanistic models could be their sophisticated control equations without available analytical solutions. Since computation cost for full fuel element simulation could be high already,it would be an extra burden to implement the time-consuming numerical method to model swelling at each FE node.Therefore,a mechanistic model with appropriate physical simplification is valuable for its analytical solvability to reduce the computation cost.The best scenario is that the mechanistic model not only satisfies the completeness of the gas mechanism,but also retains the simplicity for efficiency.

In this paper,modifications are made to the UMo swelling model [6] to improve the completeness.The effects of intergranular resolution and hydrostatic pressure are examined. The intergranular resolution modeling and its analytical approach are based on Forsberg and Massih's work [4]. Efforts have been made to further simplify its long-term approximation to enhance the efficiency.The influence of the pertinent parameters on gas bubble swelling will be investigated.Comparisons of the simulation results with the measured data are given. The computation time consumed by applying the mechanistic model in FE simulation is compared with that consumed by applying the empirical model.

## 4.3. Parametric study

We would like to first focus on the intergranular resolution rate. UMo alloy fuel is sandwiched by Al alloy cladding to form a monolithic fuel plate.The mini-plate geometry has been detailed in Fig.1 of Ref.[24].The fission condition is assumed to be uniform. ## 5.4.FE results and discussion

For the full-size plate simulation,the computation cost of FE analysis could be substantially high. Furthermore,if mechanical constraint is imposed,mesh refinement is required near the constraint side to account for the stress concentration. Therefore,it isnecessary to examine the efficiency of fission swelling subroutines.

The simulations are conducted by using three i5-4440 processors.During the FE calculation, standard steps such as spatial discretization,the assembling of the stiffness matrix and the factorization of the sparse matrix could all consume a portion of CPU time.Since the inelastic material properties such as plasticity and creep are involved,ABAQUS will carry out the stress update plus the necessary iteration steps.The subroutine incorporated for the swelling is called inside the stress iteration loop at each fuel FE node.In our simulation,the main load is the swelling of UMo fuel.

In Fig.7(a), the pressure dependence lowers the total swelling by about 7-10% at the fission density f= 6.6 × 10²7 fission/m. The distribution of swelling varies considerably between the two cases.Without pressure dependence,the distribution of the fission swelling stems from the temperature field.The fuel is cooled by the surrounding cladding and thus temperature is the highest in the middle.The highest fuel temperatures are 445 K for Fig.7(a) and 449 K for Fig. 7(b). The lowest fuel temperatures are 348 K for Fig.7(a) and 349 K for Fig.7(b). As for the mechanistic model with pressure dependence,the distribution of swelling is also subject to the pressure distribution.In Fig.7(a),we further investigated the surface pressure around the maximum swelling position (arrowed in Fig. 7(a)).We found that the local pressures right at the maximum swelling position,relying on the direction of the output path, are about 2O-4O% lower than that in the surrounding area.With that much reduction of pressure in this position, the swelling highland forms despite for a relatively low temperature.Assuming the radiation effect does not severely alter the mechanical property of the two metal materials,the fracture theory for porous metal could still be applied. The commonly used Gurson model [29] and its extended versions predict that the increasing porosity will lower the equivalent stress to enable a large inelastic deformation. Since the porosity influence on the material equations has not been considered in the current simulations, the local deformation could be considerablyunderestimated.

## 6. Conclusion

Based on Rest's mechanistic swelling model for UMo fuel, modifications are introduced to include plausible fission gas mechanisms. For one thing， the intergranular resolution has been included in the swelling model. Based on Massih and Forsberg's contributions,efforts have been made to further simplify the long term approximation.Thus,analytical expression of the mechanistic model is acquired.For another,the pressure dependence has been examined. It lowers the gaseous swelling to some extent,especially under high fission density.The Rest UMo swelling model has been incorporated in the FE simulation and has demonstrated both efficiency and reliability.The simulation results are in line with the experimental data. The introduction of the pressure dependence is an attempt to bridge the gap between fission gas mechanism and the mechanical feedback from the structure analysis.
