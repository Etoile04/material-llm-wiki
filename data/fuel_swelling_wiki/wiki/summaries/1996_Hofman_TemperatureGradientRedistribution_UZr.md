# Summary: Temperature Gradient Driven Constituent Redistribution in U-Zr Alloys

**Authors:** G.L. Hofman, S.L. Hayes, M.C. Petri (1996)  
**Journal:** Journal of Nuclear Materials 227, 277–286

## English Summary

Hofman et al. presented the first definitive observation and mechanistic model of constituent redistribution in binary U-Zr fuel during irradiation in EBR-II (Experiment X447). Two fuel elements (DP-81 and DP-11, U-22±1 at% Zr) were examined at 5 and 10 at.% peak burnup. Both developed three concentric zones: a porous Zr-enriched γ-phase central zone, a Zr-depleted intermediate δ+γ zone, and an outer α+δ zone. Unlike U-Pu-Zr fuels, Zr migrated only toward the fuel center (up the temperature gradient), with no evidence of outward migration at lower temperatures. A thermo-transport model based on Soret diffusion was developed, incorporating phase-dependent interdiffusion coefficients, partial molal enthalpies of solution, and heats of transport. The model predicted that redistribution occurs only above 935 K. A heat of transport of −50 to −100 kJ/mol for the γ-phase yielded the best agreement with experimental microprobe data. Notably, out-of-pile diffusion coefficients required a tenfold enhancement to match experimental observations, attributed to irradiation-enhanced diffusion mechanisms. Zone boundary temperatures from the phase diagram (965 K for γ/δ+γ and 935 K for δ+γ/α+δ) served as useful temperature markers for reconstructing radial temperature profiles.

## 中文摘要

Hofman等人首次明确观测并建立了U-Zr二元金属燃料在EBR-II辐照期间组元再分布的机理模型（实验X447）。两个燃料元件（DP-81和DP-11，U-22±1 at% Zr）分别在5和10 at.%峰值燃耗下检验。两者均形成三个同心区：多孔富Zr γ相中心区、贫Zr中间δ+γ区和外缘α+δ区。与U-Pu-Zr燃料不同，Zr仅向燃料中心迁移（沿温度梯度向上），在低温区未发现向外迁移的证据。建立了基于Soret扩散的热输运模型，包含相依赖的互扩散系数、偏摩尔溶解焓和输运热。模型预测仅在935 K以上发生再分布。γ相输运热为−50至−100 kJ/mol时与实验微探针数据吻合最好。值得注意的是，堆外扩散系数需要增强十倍才能匹配实验观测，这归因于辐照增强扩散机制。相图中的相界温度（965 K对应γ/δ+γ，935 K对应δ+γ/α+δ）可作为重建径向温度分布的有效温度标记。

## Key Parameters
- Fuel composition: U-22±1 at% Zr
- Peak burnup: 5 and 10 at.%
- Heat of transport (γ): −50 to −100 kJ/mol
- Redistribution threshold: >935 K
- Diffusion enhancement factor: ~10× vs out-of-pile
- Fuel radius: 2.17 mm, diameter 4.34 mm
- Linear power: 240–250 W/cm
- Surface temperature: 627–637°C
