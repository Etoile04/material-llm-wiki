## Bubble Size Distribution (R2R010)
- N = 319 bubbles measured
- Mean diameter: $3.52 \pm 0.03$ nm
- Distribution approximately Gaussian; range ~2.5–5.5 nm

## High Fission Density Microstructure (R3R050 high, $5.2\times10^{21}$ fiss/cm³)
- Subdivided grain size: **100–500 nm**
- Fission gas bubbles: **0.1–1.0 μm**, uniformly distributed
- Solid fission products decorate interior of micron-sized bubbles
- Residual [[wiki/entities/GasBubbleSuperlattice|superlattice]] persists in grains >500 nm
- No significant Mo content difference between residual and subdivided grains (TEM EDS)

## Key Findings

1. **FCC superlattice in BCC matrix**: First reported non-isomorphic bubble superlattice
2. **Bubble size saturation**: ~3.1–3.6 nm diameter by $3.0\times10^{21}$ fiss/cm³, no growth to $4.5\times10^{21}$
3. **Fission rate and temperature independence** (91–130°C, 3.8–6.6 × $10^{14}$ fiss/cm³·s): neither strongly affects superlattice size
4. **Grain subdivision threshold**: ~$4.5\times10^{21}$ fiss/cm³ triggers transition to subdivided grains + large intergranular bubbles
5. **Grain stability drives superlattice stability**: persists in grains >500 nm
6. **Solid FP precipitation only after grain subdivision**: below $4.5\times10^{21}$, solid FP in solution (single atoms/<2 nm clusters)

### Open Questions
- Exact saturation threshold (between 1.3 and 3.0 × $10^{21}$ fiss/cm³)?
- Why does monolithic U–10 wt%Mo delay breakaway swelling to ~$7\times10^{21}$ fiss/cm³?
- Are superlattice bubbles faceted? (TEM projection artifacts inconclusive)
- Gas composition inside superlattice bubbles? (EDS inconclusive for small bubbles)
