# Miller 2015 — Bubble Superlattice Structure and Formation Mechanisms

**Source:** [[wiki/summaries/2015_Miller_TEMGasBubbleSuperlatticeU7Mo|Miller 2015 Index]]

---

## Bubble Superlattice Formation

During irradiation of U–7 wt%Mo dispersion fuel, fission gas (Xe, Kr) atoms nucleate into nanoscale bubbles that self-organize into a periodic **[[wiki/entities/GasBubbleSuperlattice|superlattice]]** within grain interiors.

The superlattice has a **face-centered cubic (FCC) structure** coherent with the host U–7 wt%Mo **body-centered cubic (BCC)** matrix ($a = 0.343$ nm). This FCC-in-BCC relationship is the **first reported case** of its kind; ion-irradiated materials typically form superlattices isomorphic with the host.

Ordering extends over **micron-length scales** across entire fuel grains — much longer range than ion-implantation superlattices (tens of nm).

### Three Proposed Formation Mechanisms (from literature)

1. Elastic interaction between bubbles
2. Planar diffusion of host interstitial atoms
3. Dislocation loop punching from over-pressurized bubbles

The dominant mechanism is further investigated in [[wiki/summaries/2016_Hu_GasBubbleSuperlatticeFormationPF|Hu 2016 (phase-field modeling)]], which identifies **fast 1-D migration of self-interstitial atoms (SIAs)** along ⟨110⟩ directions as the key driver — not elastic interaction.

## Bubble Superlattice Saturation

At fission densities between **3.0 and 4.5 × $10^{21}$ fiss/cm³**, superlattice bubble diameter is **saturated** at ~3.1–3.6 nm.

Additional fission gas from increasing burnup accumulates preferentially along **grain boundaries** rather than growing intragranular superlattice bubbles.

---

## Grain Sub-Division and Superlattice Collapse

At fission density **~$4.5\times10^{21}$ fiss/cm³**, U–7 wt%Mo undergoes **grain subdivision**:
- Grains refine to **100–500 nm** diameter
- Subdivided grains **cannot support** the ordered superlattice
- Residual superlattice persists only in grains >500 nm
- Fission gas from collapsed superlattice coalesces into **0.1–1.0 μm** bubbles uniformly distributed throughout the fuel
- **Solid fission products** precipitate on interior surfaces of these micron-sized bubbles (not observed in bulk at lower fission densities)

### Proposed Grain Sub-Division Mechanism

Analogous to **rim structure [[wiki/entities/Recrystallization|recrystallization]]** in UO₂:
- Driven by stored solution energy of fission products (single atoms/defect clusters <2 nm at low temperatures)
- Xe in UO₂: ~9.5 eV solution energy → ~20 kJ/mol at 2.4 at.% Xe → comparable to cold-worked metal recrystallization threshold (~20 J/mol)
- When fission product concentration exceeds what the U–Mo lattice can store, grain subdivision reduces internal energy by increasing grain boundary area
- Low irradiation temperatures (<150°C) → low defect mobility → high stored energy accumulation

---

## Bubble vs. Void Superlattice Classification

Based on $T_{\text{irr}}/T_m = 0.26$–$0.30$ (below void formation range 0.3–0.6 $T_m$, $T_m = 1404$ K):
- Classified as **bubble superlattice** (fission gas stabilizes bubbles)
- Not void superlattice (which forms at higher $T/T_m$)
