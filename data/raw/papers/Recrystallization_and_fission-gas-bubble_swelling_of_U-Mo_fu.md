Article history:                                        At high burnup, U–Mo fuel exhibits some form of recrystallization, by which fuel grains are subdivided.
Received 4 August 2012                                  The effect of grain subdivision is to effectively enhance ﬁssion gas bubble (FGB) swelling due to increased
Accepted 8 January 2013                                 grain boundaries. Inter-granular FGB swelling, i.e., FGB formation and growth at the grain boundaries, is
Available online 20 January 2013
                                                        much larger than the intra-granular FGB swelling. Recrystallized fuel volume fractions of U–Mo fuels irra-
                                                        diated to ﬁssion densities reaching 5.7  1021 f/cm3 were measured. Analytical expressions of recrystal-
                                                        lization kinetics of U–Mo fuel during irradiation have been developed through the usage of the Avrami
                                                        equation, a phenomenological equation which is also used to describe similar typical transformation
                                                        reactions, such as new phase formation. In this work, we present a novel FGB swelling model of U–Mo
                                                        fuel that is expressed in terms of Mo content, extent of cold work (fuel powder fabrication method),
                                                        and ﬁssion density.
