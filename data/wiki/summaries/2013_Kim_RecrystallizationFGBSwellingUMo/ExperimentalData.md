| Fuel Type | Full Recrystallization Threshold |

| Low Mo alloys (U-6Mo, U-7Mo) | Earlier at same fission density |

## Key Findings

1. **[[wiki/entities/Recrystallization|Recrystallization]] is the key driver of FGB swelling enhancement** — post-rx rate (~5.2%) is 8.7× pre-rx rate (~0.6%) per $10^{21}$ f/cm³
2. **Avrami equation** ($n = 2.6$) describes U-Mo recrystallization kinetics; comparable to Rest [14] analytical model ($n \approx 3$)
3. **Manufacturing process matters** — ground powder recrystallizes faster than atomized (lower $F_0$: 1.38 vs. 1.67×$10^{21}$ f/cm³) due to higher cold work and dislocation density
4. **Mo content affects rate** — lower Mo → faster recrystallization ($K \propto (10 - x_{Mo})$)
5. **Fission rate and temperature effects negligible** — in typical research reactor conditions (<250 °C)
6. **U-Mo vs. UO₂**: UO₂ recrystallizes earlier (~3.1×$10^{21}$ f/cm³) — possibly due to higher defect mobility in ceramics
