## a b s t r a c t

It is hopeful that fusion-fission hybrid energy system will become an effective approach to achieve longterm sustainable development of fission energy. U-10Zr alloy (which means the mass ratio of Zr is 10%) fuel is the key material of subcritical blanket for fusion-fission hybrid energy system which the irradiation performance need to be considered. Indirect neutron radiography is used to detect the irradiated U-10Zr alloy because of the high residual dose in this paper. Different burnup samples (0.1%, 0.3%, 0.5% and 0.7%) have been tested with a special indirect neutron radiography device at CMRR (China Mianyang Research Reactor). The resolution of the device is better than 50 mm and the quantitative analysis of swelling behaviors was carried out. The results show that the swelling behaviors relate well to burnup character which can be detected accurately by indirect neutron radiography.

## 1. Introduction

Energy is not only the foundation of modern society development, but also the provisory condition. In order to insure the economic and national safety, nuclear energy is confirmed as the important way of national energy stratagem. Because fusion nuclear energy is immature, fission nuclear energy is used as the main commercial nuclear energy but the efficiency is low and spent fuel is hard to dispose. It is hopeful that fusion-fission hybrid energy system will become an effective approach to achieve longterm sustainable development of fission energy [1]. Metallic fuel which used in subcritical blanket is very significant for fusionfission hybrid energy system. Researches show that U-Zr alloy may become the most potential material for metallic fuel [2–5]. As the key material of blanket, U-Zr alloy which works in a high temperature, high pressure and radioactive environment need a nondestructive detection to insure reliability. But the irradiated U-Zr alloy fuel has high radioactivity and is hard to detect by routine methods.

Neutron radiography, which is a Non-Destructive Testing technology and complement to x-ray radiography, is suitable for investigating the defects in low-Z materials shielded by high-Z materials. Direct neutron radiography (DNR) records the imaging results at neutron flux, which is suitable for normal samples. Indirect neutron radiography (INR), which records the imaging results with neutron flux offline, can be applied to test samples with high radioactivity [6–8].

The material performance and fabrication technology of U-10Zr alloy is under researching at Chinese Academy of Engineering Physics (CAEP). In order to obtain the inner information of irradiated U-10Zr alloy, INR is used to detect different burnup samples. A special INR device has been manufactured to image and transport the high radioactivity samples.

## 2. Method

Differ with DNR, INR, which means double exposure, contains two steps: exposing the neutron converter (such as In/Dy foils) in the neutron beam and contacting the converter with imaging recorder (such as imaging plate and film) clingy to take the second exposure (Fig. 1).

## 4. INR device

The irradiated samples are stored at the spent fuel pool which can be transported to hot cell by mechanical facility. Then the high radioactivity samples should be moved from hot cell to the end of neutron beam line to expose by person. So a device is needed to image and transport the high radioactivity fuel samples.

An INR device has been design and built based the CMRR cold neutron beam line which used to keep the sample shielding well during transport and person operation. The device contains three parts: transport case, imaging case and bracket. The sample is moved from hot cell to the end of beam line by transport case, and then dropped to the imaging case by a telemechanics guide apparatus to expose.

The INR device is located at the end of CMRR C1 tube. The L/D of the beam is nearly 325 and the sample -converter distance is 5 mm (Fig. 3).

The wall of transport case and imaging case is made of Pb and Fe. The Pb thickness of imaging case is 20 cm and the exterior gamma dose is 11.4 μSv/h(calculated by Geant4) when a sample (burnup 0.7%) dropped to the imaging case.

## 5. Experiments

The INR experiments are carried when the reactor power is 15 MW. The neutron converter is a 100 mm Indium metal foil which the size is 100 mm  100 mm. The first exposure time is 1 h and the second exposure time is 4 h. A Kodak AA400 film is used as the imaging recorder. The film data are digitized by a film scanner Array-2905 and the resolution is 50 mm.

A neutron radiographic sensitivity indicator is used to test the imaging resolution of the INR device. Fig. 4 shows the INR result of the indicator and the 50 mm slot can be distinguish clearly.

The INR results of the different type fuel samples are shown in Figs. 5 and 6. The white area in the middle of the samples is the U-10Zr fuel pin which is the most absorbing component. If there is no swelling behavior, the shape of the fuel pin imaging results should be rectangular. But now the shape is expanded in Figs. 5 and 6.

![](images/9e658e99a3f49d92a307f67e32224ce6fc764f042ab58529039d3c832bba1311.jpg)  
Fig. 6. The INR results of type B fuel samples.

The swelling behavior can be observed obviously in sample No. 5, 10 and 11. The edge of No. 5 sample seems to be spread which shown in Fig. 7. The gray value outside the fuel pin of line A is higher than line B and line C which means the fuel pin may broken and the U-10Zr alloy has expanded to Al powder shell.

The quantitative analysis has been carried out to investigate the radial and axial swelling behaviors of different burnup samples which shown in Fig. 8.

The quantitative results of type A show the swelling rate is rise as the burnup rise. The fuel pin swelling phenomena is obvious by INR when burnup is higher than 0.5%, and the radial behaviors and axial behaviors are different. The radial swelling rate is beyond 10% but axial swelling rate is only around 2% when burnup is 0.7%. The quantitative results of type B show the swelling rate is lower than type A even the maximal swelling rate is only 1.75%. The inner structure of the samples may cause the different swelling behaviors of type A and type B. There is a Zr alloy shell outside the fuel pin of type B which can press the pin and reduce the swelling rate.

Sample No. 11 has been anatomized and observed with metallurgical microscope to check the results of INR. The result shows that the fuel pin edge is spread which is correspond with INR (Fig. 9).

## 6. Conclusion

The swelling behavior of irradiated U-10Zr alloy fuel has been detected by INR at CMRR of CAEP. The special INR device has been developed to transport the sample and keep safety. The quantitative analysis shows that INR is suitable to detect the radioactive sample and the swelling rate is rise as the burnup rise. The radial behaviors and axial behaviors are different and the radial swelling rate is high than axial swelling rate when burnup rise. The metallurgical microscope result of sample also shows the swelling behavior of fuel pin edge which is correspond with INR. In the future, imaging plate will be used to record the imaging instead of film for the less exposure time, low activation and linear response. Moreover, the human exposure risk is much lower when using imaging plate.
