# Summary: Constituent Redistribution in U–Pu–Zr Fuel During Irradiation

**Authors:** Y.S. Kim, G.L. Hofman, S.L. Hayes, Y.H. Sohn (2004)  
**Journal:** Journal of Nuclear Materials 327, 27–36

## English Summary

Kim et al. analyzed constituent redistribution in U–Pu–Zr metallic fuel using postirradiation examination data from EBR-II fuel element T179 (U-19Pu-10Zr wt%, 1.9 at.% burnup). The fuel exhibited three concentric zones: a Zr-enriched central γ-phase zone (42 at.% Zr at center, up from 23 at.% initial), a Zr-depleted intermediate γ+ζ zone, and a slightly Zr-enriched outer δ+ζ zone. Phase boundaries were estimated at 650°C (γ/(γ+ζ)) and 600°C ((γ+ζ)/(δ+ζ)). Using a Boltzmann-parameter-based analytical method, the authors calculated interdiffusion fluxes for U, Pu, and Zr without requiring ternary interdiffusion coefficients. From these fluxes, effective interdiffusion coefficients and heats of transport were extracted for each phase region. The model predictions matched measured concentration profiles well. Zr migrated toward the fuel center in γ and most of γ+ζ phases, while U behaved nearly oppositely. Pu showed minimal redistribution with slight central migration. The study also examined minor actinide additions (Am, Np): Am migrated similarly to Zr with local precipitation in porous regions, while Np behaved like Pu. A key finding was that porosity buildup from swelling inhibited diffusion more than irradiation enhanced it.

## 中文摘要

Kim等人利用EBR-II辐照实验中T179燃料元件（U-19Pu-10Zr wt%，燃耗1.9 at.%）的辐照后检验数据，分析了U-Pu-Zr金属燃料的组元再分布。燃料呈现三个同心区域：富Zr中心γ相区（中心Zr浓度42 at.%，初始为23 at.%）、贫Zr中间γ+ζ区和外缘略富Zr的δ+ζ区。相界温度估计为650°C（γ/(γ+ζ)）和600°C（(γ+ζ)/(δ+ζ)）。采用Boltzmann参数解析方法，无需三元互扩散系数即可计算U、Pu和Zr的互扩散通量，进而提取各相区的有效互扩散系数和输运热。模型预测与实测浓度分布吻合良好。Zr在γ和大部分γ+ζ区向中心迁移，U行为近乎相反。Pu再分布很小。研究还发现少量次锕系元素（Am和Np）的添加不影响U、Pu和Zr的再分布特征：Am迁移行为类似Zr并在多孔区析出，Np行为类似Pu。肿胀引起的孔隙率增加对扩散的抑制作用超过辐照增强效应。

## Key Parameters
- Fuel composition: U-19Pu-10Zr (wt%), 61U-16Pu-23Zr (at.%)
- Burnup: 1.9 at.%
- Linear heat rate: 420 W/cm
- Phase boundary temperatures: 650°C, 600°C
- Three zone boundaries at r/R = 0.32 and 0.58
