                                                             phase boundary in U-10Mo irradiated to low fission density is wavy and periodic in morphology and that GBS

                                                             high burnup structure development may initiate first at grain boundaries and UC/U-Mo interfaces.

2. Materials and methods

    The U-10Mo fuel specimen investigated in this study was irradiated
as a part of the MP1 irradiation experiments. The U-10Mo fuel cross-
section is identified by KGT-3706 with an average fission density of
~1.6 × 1021 fissions/cm3. TEM lamellae samples were obtained from
the midplane of the fuel cross-section where the local fission density in
~1.30 × 1021 fissions/cm3. The TEM lamellae sample each measuring
20 µm × 10 µm × 100 nm were prepared using the FEI quanta focused
ion beam scanning electron microscope (FIB/SEM). Samples from each
fuel plate were prepared by depositing platinum (Pt) protective layers of
20 µm × 2 µm × 2 µm at 10 kV and 0.2nA near the fuel midplane. The
samples were then coarse-trenched to obtain using the focused ion beam
in-situ lift-out system at 10 kV and 6nA and later mounted on a copper
(Cu) grid. Following mounting, the samples were iteratively thinned on
