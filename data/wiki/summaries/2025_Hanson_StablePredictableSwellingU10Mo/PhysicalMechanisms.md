# Stable and Predictable Swelling U-10Mo — Physical Mechanisms

*Sub-page of [[2025_Hanson_StablePredictableSwellingU10Mo]]*

---

## Fission Gas Bubble Nucleation and Growth by Fission Density Regime

### Low Fission Density (< 3 × 10²¹ fissions/cm³)
- Micron-scale fission gas bubbles precipitate **exclusively at grain boundaries**
- The fission gas inventory interior to grains is retained in an **FCC gas superlattice**
- Original grain boundaries serve as primary sinks and precipitation sites

### Intermediate Fission Density (~3 × 10²¹ fissions/cm³)
- **Grain refinement begins** from original grain boundaries, forming a high burn-up structure (HBS)
- Linked to collapse of the fission gas superlattice
- Coalescence of additional micron-scale fission gas porosity in newly formed sub-grain boundaries

### High Fission Density (> 5 × 10²¹ fissions/cm³)
- HBS becomes the primary phase with small islands of original grains remaining (C-plates) or fully refined HBS (B-plates)
- Fission gas porosity forms into larger bubbles
- **Very limited bubble interconnection** observed — stable swelling regime confirmed

---

## Role of Mo Homogenization

### Homogenized C-plates (Commercial Fabrication + Anneal)
- Uniform Mo distribution → expected microstructural evolution with fission density
- Standard progression: grain boundary precipitation → grain refinement → HBS

### Non-homogenized B-plates (Baseline Fabrication, No Anneal)
- Mo chemical banding → regions of low Mo content decompose to less stable α-U-Mo phase
- Irradiation-assisted reformation to γ-U-Mo produces **bands of micron-scale grains** at lower fission densities
- Increased grain boundary density → early precipitation of micron-scale fission gas porosity
- **Offset of swelling regime to lower fission densities**

---

## Swelling Constraint Mechanism

In plate-type fuel geometries:
- Constraint in **axial length** and **transverse width** results in volumetric fuel swelling being directly correlated to **change in plate thickness**
- This geometric constraint simplifies swelling measurement from thickness maps

## Key Microstructural Observations by Condition

| Condition | C-plates (homogenized) | B-plates (non-homogenized) |
|-----------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| Low FD (< 3 × 10²¹ f/cm³) | Fission gas bubbles at grain boundaries only | Micron-scale grains in low-Mo bands; early porosity |
| Moderate FD (~3–5 × 10²¹ f/cm³) | Grain refinement starting at boundaries | Advanced refinement; larger HBS porosity |
| High FD (~5–6 × 10²¹ f/cm³) | Partial HBS with remaining grain islands | Fully refined HBS; larger fission gas pores |
| Bubble interconnection | Very limited | Very limited |
